About the Hackathon

Afflux: Build for Impact is a 48-hour online programming hackathon that brings together developers, innovators, and problem-solvers to create software solutions for real-world challenges.

The hackathon encourages participants to transform ideas into impactful applications through programming. Whether it's improving everyday life, increasing productivity, enhancing accessibility, supporting education, advancing healthcare, or addressing any other meaningful challenge, every solution should be driven by the goal of creating real value for users.

Rather than focusing on a specific technology or domain, Afflux emphasizes innovation, creativity, technical excellence, and practical problem-solving. Participants are encouraged to think beyond prototypes and develop software that has the potential to make a genuine difference.

Afflux is more than just a competition—it's an opportunity to learn, collaborate, showcase your skills, and contribute to building technology that creates a positive impact.

Eligibility

Afflux: Build for Impact is open to anyone with an interest in programming, software development, and innovation.

Whether you are a student, working professional, freelancer, or self-taught developer, you are welcome to participate. Participants from all countries and experience levels are encouraged to join.

No prior hackathon experience is required. Whether you are building your first project or your fiftieth, this hackathon is an opportunity to learn, collaborate, and showcase your skills.

Team Formation

Participants may compete individually or as a team.

  • Individual participation is allowed.
  • Teams may have up to 4 members.
  • Each participant may join only one team.
  • Team members are free to divide responsibilities according to their strengths, such as frontend development, backend development, AI, UI/UX design, testing, or documentation.

All submissions must be made under the registered participant or team before the submission deadline.

Timeline

The hackathon will be conducted over 48 continuous hours in an online format.

The complete event schedule, including registration, hackathon start, submission deadline, judging period, result announcement, and certificate distribution, will be announced before the event begins.

Participants are advised to plan their work efficiently and submit their final projects before the official deadline. Late submissions may not be considered for evaluation.

What You Can Build

Participants are free to build any programming-based software project that solves a real-world problem or provides meaningful value to users.

There are no restrictions on the programming language, framework, technology stack, or development environment you choose. You are encouraged to select the tools that best suit your idea.

Examples of eligible projects include, but are not limited to:

Web Applications Mobile Applications Desktop Applications Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning Applications APIs and Backend Systems Developer Tools Automation Software Cybersecurity Solutions Educational Platforms Healthcare Applications Financial Technology (FinTech) Software Accessibility Solutions Cloud-Based Applications Open Source Software Productivity Tools Purpose-driven Games

Any other programming-based software project that delivers real-world value.

Projects must involve software development and programming. Hardware-only projects, non-technical concepts without implementation, or submissions unrelated to programming are not eligible.

Submission Requirements

All project submissions must be completed through the official Google Form, which will be shared with participants before the submission deadline.

Each submission must include the following:

  • Project Name
  • Participant or Team Name
  • Email Address
  • Contact Number
  • Project Description
  • GitHub Repository Link
  • Deployed Project Link (if applicable)
  • Demo Video Link (YouTube – Public or Unlisted)

Participants are responsible for ensuring that all submitted links are publicly accessible and functional before the submission deadline. Incomplete or inaccessible submissions may not be considered for evaluation.

Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

  • Innovation – The originality and creativity of the idea.
  • Real-World Impact – The extent to which the solution addresses a genuine problem and provides meaningful value.
  • Technical Implementation – The quality, functionality, and completeness of the software solution.

Judges will evaluate projects based on the submitted materials, including the project description, source code, deployed application (if available), and demonstration video.

The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Rules & Guidelines

  • All projects must be developed primarily during the hackathon period.
  • Participants may use any programming language, framework, or technology stack.
  • AI-assisted development tools such as ChatGPT, Gemini, GitHub Copilot, and similar tools are permitted to assist with learning, brainstorming, debugging, and improving productivity.
  • Every participant or team must fully understand and be able to explain the code and solution they submit. Plagiarism, copying existing projects, or falsely representing someone else's work as your own may result in disqualification.
  • Open-source libraries, frameworks, and APIs may be used in accordance with their respective licenses.
  • Participants must ensure that all submitted information and project links are accurate and accessible.
  • Participants retain full ownership of their projects. By participating, they grant Afflux permission to feature their project, screenshots, descriptions, and demo videos for promotional, educational, and showcase purposes, with appropriate credit to the creators.
  • The organizers reserve the right to disqualify any submission that violates these rules or compromises the fairness and integrity of the hackathon.
  • The decision of the organizers and judges shall be final in all matters related to the hackathon.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is participation free?

Yes. This hackathon is completely free to join.

Who can participate?

Anyone with an interest in programming and software development is welcome.

Can I participate individually?

Yes. You may participate individually or as part of a team of up to four members.

Can I use AI tools such as ChatGPT or GitHub Copilot?

Yes. AI-assisted tools are allowed. However, participants must understand, verify, and take responsibility for all submitted work. Plagiarism or misrepresentation of work is not permitted.

Can I use open-source libraries and frameworks?

Yes. You may use publicly available libraries and frameworks, provided their licenses allow it.

Is there any restriction on programming languages or technologies?

No. You are free to use any programming language, framework, or technology stack.

Do I need to deploy my project?

Deployment is recommended whenever possible, but if your project cannot reasonably be deployed, provide clear instructions and a demonstration video.

Will every participant receive a certificate?

Eligible participants who submit a valid project will receive a Participation Certificate, while the Top 3 projects will receive Winner Certificates.

Where will the submission form be available?

The official Google Form will be shared with all participants before the submission deadline.

How will winners be announced?

Winners will be announced through the official Afflux communication channels and website after the judging process is completed.
